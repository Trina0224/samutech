Our Technology

It was estimated that the yearly loss due to wear and corrosion in the US is approximately $500 billion, which is about the total revenues of 4 top companies (Apple, Google, IBM, and Intel) in 2018. Material’s surface quality directly impacts its wear and corrosion resistance.

Our surface modification techniques can enhance the following characteristics of material surface:

  • Roughness
  • Hardness
  • Wear resistance
  • Corrosion resistance
  • Fatigue strength

Surface modification technology is widely used in manufacturing, automotive, medical, and energy industries. In the US, there are around 10,000 establishments (including automotive, aircraft, power, and construction industries) who depend on engineered surfaces with support from around 25,000 industries.




Diamond is the hardest natural substance found in earth. Diamond-like carbon (DLC) can be coated on various materials to make their surface as hard as diamond and greatly enhance their wear resistance.


Diamond-like carbon film(DLC) with Diamond SP3 and graphite SP2 structure has excellent mechanical property,

  • Smooth
  • Insulation
  • Super hardness
  • Corrosion resistance
  • Good abrasion resistance(low friction and wear)
  • Anti-oxygen, water, ultraviolet
  • Biological affinity

DLC coating typically requires vacuum environment, which is expensive and has limit on application. Our technology can generate DLC with atmospheric plasma, and has broader applications and cost advantage.


About Us

SAMU Technology was established in May 2018 in Fremont, the eastern hub of Silicon Valley, USA. The four co-founders combined have more than 100 years of high-tech R&D and management experience. The company’s current core team consists of 8 members with 5 Ph.D. and 3 master’s degrees, mainly from the United States, China, and Japan. Each member has more than 18 years of experience in the high-tech industries. The 5 key members of the company’s technical team specialize in material science application and semiconductor equipment development.





The company’s core technology includes nanometer-scale surface modification, as well as the world-leading air plasma sputtering. The core technology can significantly improve material’s surface hardness, corrosion resistance and wear resistance. The air plasma technology greatly reduces the cost of the last generation of plasma technology that requires a vacuum. The company’s core technology is protected by patents and has high entry threshold. It has great commercial value from ordinary consumer products such as smartphones to cutting-edge technology including aerospace. The company’s targeting markets include automobiles, smart devices, medical equipment, and non-toxic food sterilization. The company has established initial strategic partnerships with a list of automotive, mobile, medical, and food hygiene companies in the US and Japan. The company plans to offer the first product by the end of this year (2019).
